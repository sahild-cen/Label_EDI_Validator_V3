Definition of EDI 
EDI is the computer to computer exchange of business transactions between organizations in an agreed 
upon format. "Computer-to-computer" means that the data sent or received is communicated via 
electronic transmission, without human intervention or interpretation.  
 
Maintaining standardized formats is the foundation of a Global EDI service. EDI requires business 
partners to follow the standards as defined by the format layout and content held within. After integrating 
onto the EDI platform, data will be converted by EDI translation programs into the exact same format and 
provided by each country. Industry standard formats have been defined by EDIFACT, ANSI and 
RosettaNet.

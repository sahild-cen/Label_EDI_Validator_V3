Introduction 
 
This specification provides the definition of the global standard DHL manifesting EDI messages. This is a 
guide to be used in satisfying the requirements when exchanging electronic data to become a certified 
trading partner with DHL and its partners. The data contained in these EDI messages consist of 
manifesting information related to the transportation of cargo and transport related charges.  
 
For purposes of this document, “Charges”  means all applicable transportation and other charges, 
including but not limited to, all applicable accessorial charges, surcharges, additional handling charges, 
late payment fees, and collection costs including collection agency fees, legal fees, court costs, credit 
reports, and bad check collection fees, in accordance with applicable laws.   
 
Notwithstanding any billing plan that is in effect or payment or billing option selected at the time of 
shipment, the shipper is ultimately liable for and agrees to pay all Charges, including in the event of 
insolvency, bankruptcy, or non-payment or refusal to pay by the consignee or third party. 
 
All Charges must be ‘paid in full’ (per the invoice), in the lawful currency noted as the billing currency.

Definition of EDI 
EDI is the computer to computer exchange of business transactions between organizations in an agreed 
upon format. "Computer-to-computer" means that the data sent or received is communicated via 
electronic transmission, without human intervention or interpretation.  
 
Maintaining standardized formats is the foundation of a Global EDI service. EDI requires business 
partners to follow the standards as defined by the format layout and content held within. After integrating 
onto the EDI platform, data will be converted by EDI translation programs into the exact same format and 
provided by each country. Industry standard formats have been defined by EDIFACT, ANSI and 
RosettaNet.

IFCSUM Description 
This document is part of the document “EDI Implementation Guide for customer interfaces”. It provides 
the definition of the International Forwarding and Consolidation Summary Message (IFCSUM) to be used 
in Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) between Deutsche Post World Net partners and its customers. 
 
Therefore, this specification can be used for EDI exchange between customers and Deutsche Post 
subsidiaries. The Deutsche Post World Net partner takes care of the EDI exchange to DHL Express and 
other partners in this group. 
 
Within this document the IFCSUM declaration of data elements will be described along with branching 
diagrams to show the arrangement of segments and segment groups. It also contains detailed 
descriptions of segment and assignment information for individual data elements.

Mandatory/Optional Fields 
 
M Mandatory - This item must be present in all data transactions - used for critical  
data which is also Mandatory in the EDIFACT standard. 
 
R Required - This item must be present in all data transactions. In the opinion of the implementation 
guide designers it is essential for this business use of the transaction set, however not defined as 
Mandatory in the EDIFACT standard.  
 
C Conditional - This item is the subject of a syntax rule (a relational condition) which links its usage with 
that of other items. If not used a data element separator giving its position must still be given, unless it 
appears at the end of a segment in which case the data element separator is also not required. 
 
X Not Used - This item is not used in a data transaction. A data element separator giving its position must 
still be given, but no content should be given, unless it appears at the end of a segment in which case the 
data element separator is also not required.

Units of Measure 
The following codes are valid for unit of measurement for weight, volume and dimensions 
Weight 
Default = KGM (Kilograms) 
Also acceptable: LBR (Pounds) 
Volume 
Default = MTQ (Cubic Meters) 
Also acceptable: CMQ (Cubic Centimeters), INQ (Cubic Inches) 
Dimensions (Length, Width, Height) 
Default = CMT (Centimeters) 
Also acceptable: MTR (Meters), INH (Inches)

Appendix F – Return Shipments 
A “Return” shipment (a shipment of items previously exported from a country, by any carrier, now being 
returned for repair or some other reason) may, depending on the shipment destination country, need to be 
assessed differently by destination Customs. Therefore particular information may need to be provided in 
the manifest data submitted for such a shipment and on any documents also submitted, such as a 
Commercial Invoice. 
 
The Commercial Invoice may be rendered as an image file by DHL Express from the manifest data, which 
can then be made available to the shipper (please contact DHL Express if this solution is required).

5.11.4.1. 
Germany (mandatory) - background 
 
A federal law requires that, besides a clear indication of each piece’s weight on the Transport Label, additional 
specific icons or symbols be displayed, when certain conditions are met. This has the purpose of ensuring: 
• 
users become aware of a piece weight being above specific thresholds  
• 
pieces are handled safely, potentially, through the use of appropriate equipment 
 
This rule applies to all deliveries with a final destination in Germany (DE), whether they are domestic or  
international shipments. In the future, similar rules might also cover pickups and other handling processes.  
The rule does not apply to shipments in transit via Germany. 
 
5.11.4.1.1. Scope 
 
The changes will be implemented across all Transport Label templates (e.g. ECOM, NETW and DUO). 
 
5.11.4.1.2. Weight Symbols 
 
Weight Symbols  
  
  
 
 
5.11.4.1.3. Business Rules 
 
The following business logic and rules apply:  
 
1) The Weight Symbols shall be applied for all shipments delivered in Germany (DE), on all the respective 
Transport Labels 
 
2) Only “PIECE_WEIGHT” (Customer-Declared Piece Weight) will be used to determine the weight symbol 
rendering; “SHIPMENT_WEIGHT” will not be considered 
 
a) If PIECE_WEIGHT is less than 10 kg or 22 lb, no Weight Symbol will be displayed  
 
b) If PIECE_WEIGHT is equal to or greater than 10 kg or 22 lb but less than 20 kg or 44 lb, a "10+ kg" 
symbol shall be displayed on the Transport Label 
 
c) If PIECE_WEIGHT is equal or greater than 20 kg or 44 lb, a "20+ kg" symbol shall be displayed on the 
Transport Label
